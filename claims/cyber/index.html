Cyber Attacks & Data Breach Insurance Claim: What Your Business Is Owed

Ransomware, data breach, wire fraud, business email compromise. Know what's covered, what's typically denied, and how to document your claim so the insurer can't lowball you.

What's Typically Covered
  • Ransomware payment and data recovery costs
  • Business interruption from system shutdown
  • Notification costs for data breach
  • Legal defense for privacy liability
  • Wire fraud and BEC losses (with crime endorsement)
  • Credit monitoring for affected customers
What Insurers Commonly Deny
  • Social engineering without specific endorsement
  • Losses exceeding cyber policy sublimits
  • Pre-existing system vulnerabilities knowingly ignored
Documentation Checklist
Gather these before contacting your adjuster. Missing documentation is the #1 reason claims are underpaid.
  • Forensic IT investigation report
  • Documentation of ransom demand and payment
  • Customer notification records
  • Business income loss calculation during downtime
  • Incident timeline from first detection
Frequently Asked Questions
Ransomware locked my business systems for two weeks. Is that a covered business interruption?
Yes, if you have cyber insurance with business interruption coverage. Standard commercial property policies typically exclude cyber events — a standalone cyber policy is essential.
