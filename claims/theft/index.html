Theft & Burglary Insurance Claim: What Your Business Is Owed

Break-ins, robbery, employee theft, equipment theft, copper theft. Know what's covered, what's typically denied, and how to document your claim so the insurer can't lowball you.

What's Typically Covered
  • Stolen business personal property and inventory
  • Building damage from forced entry
  • Cash and securities (up to sublimit)
  • Equipment and tools theft
  • Employee dishonesty/fidelity coverage
  • Copper and metal theft damage
What Insurers Commonly Deny
  • Mysterious disappearance without evidence of theft
  • Theft by owner or principal
  • Inventory shortage without proof of theft
  • Employee theft without fidelity coverage endorsement
Documentation Checklist
Gather these before contacting your adjuster. Missing documentation is the #1 reason claims are underpaid.
  • Police report filed within 24 hours
  • Inventory list with purchase prices and serial numbers
  • Security camera footage preserved immediately
  • Photos of forced entry points
  • Prior inventory records for comparison
Frequently Asked Questions
My employee stole from the business — does commercial insurance cover that?
Standard commercial policies exclude employee theft. You need a crime policy or employee dishonesty endorsement. Fidelity bonds also cover this risk.
Someone broke into my store but nothing was taken — just damage to the door. Is that covered?
Yes. Vandalism and malicious mischief coverage (included in most commercial policies) covers damage from forced entry even if no theft occurred.
